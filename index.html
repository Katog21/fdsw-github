Chilean National Museum of Natural History

History

The museum is one of the oldest natural history museums in South America. It was founded on September 14, 1830 by the French naturalist Claudio Gay, commissioned by the Chilean government

Architecture

The current building was constructed in 1875 as a palace, or pavilion, for the Chilean International Exhibition

Address

Parque Quinta Normal, Santiago, Región Metropolitana, Chile

Departments

The five departments are botany, zoology, entomology, anthropology, and paleontology.

Museum Highlights

Dinosaur

Mesozoic era vertebrates, including a specimen of Carnotaurus sastrei

Sei whale

The Central Hall exhibits, including a 17-meter skeleton of a sei whale

Ancient

Cultural anthropology: the Aymara, Mapuche, Selk'nam, Rapanui, Kaweskar, and Yámana.
